Tuna Salad Sandwich

This is a tuna salad sandwich! It's a all-American diner classic. Cool, creamy, and refreshing tuna salad sandwiched in between two slices of toast to create a... a sandwich.

Coffee, candy, ketchup, mustard, napkins, sugar packets, and checkered backdrop not included. Chicken salad is not the opposite of tuna salad!

Ingredients

Part I: tuna salad

  • Canned tuna
  • One small onion
  • One small bell pepper
  • 80ish grams of unflavored, unsweetened Greek yogurt
  • Paprika
  • Lemon juice (the secred ingredient!)
  • Your favorite hot sauce

Part II: sandwich

  • Two slices of bread

Instructions

  1. Toast the bread to your liking. Or just eat it raw if you're into that. If you toast the bread, try to time it so that you are finished making the tuna salad as soon as the bread is becomes toast.
  2. Open the tuna can, drain out the excess liquid, and plop that fishy into a bowl.
  3. Chop up the onion. Put it in the tuna bowl.
  4. Chop up the bell pepper. Plop it in the tuna bowl.
  5. Greek yogurt time. You know where to plop it.
  6. Give it a little mix.
  7. Add the paprika, lemon juice, and hot sauce to taste.
  8. Give that mush one more mix.
  9. Put the tunaa salad on one slice of toast and then put the other slice of toast on top of the tuna salad.
  10. Enjoy!
